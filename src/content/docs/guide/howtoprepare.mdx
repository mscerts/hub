This page is going to go over different exam preparation strategies. If you are unsure on how to prepare for Microsoft exams, this is the place to start.
:::

<Tabs>
	<TabItem label="Business">
		# Business
		:::note
		Best for business users and power users, with a focus on understanding basic concepts or your ability to use an end user tool.
		:::

		These are the easier exams Microsoft offers. They are not technical, don't require any previous knowledge or experience. All information you need to know is generally in the Microsoft Learn course or it is just common sense. 

		:::tip
		Use the official study guide as your source of truth for what is in the exam. Go to https://aka.ms/examcode-studyguide (for example: https://aka.ms/ab730-studyguide). Always reference it so you stay focused on relevant exam topics and avoid getting sidetracked.
		:::

	</TabItem>

	<TabItem label="Fundamentals">
		# Fundamentals
		:::note
		Best for non-technical roles and anyone new to Microsoft Cloud products, with a focus on core concepts, key products, and common use cases.
		:::

		Slightly more technical than the Business exams, but still very entry level. These exams are great for people who are new to Microsoft products and want to get a general understanding of how they work. 
		
		They are also great for people who want to validate their knowledge of Microsoft products without having to go into too much technical detail.

		:::tip
		Use the official study guide as your source of truth for what is in the exam. Go to https://aka.ms/examcode-studyguide (for example: https://aka.ms/ab900-studyguide). Always reference it so you stay focused on relevant exam topics and avoid getting sidetracked.
		:::

	</TabItem>

	<TabItem label="Associate">
		# Associate
		:::note
		Best for working professionals who want to validate product skills, with a focus on deeper technical knowledge and practical implementation.
		:::

		:::caution
		Associate exams are role-based and test practical skills, not just course memorization. They can be very difficult without real hands-on experience with the products.
		:::

		:::tip
		Use the official study guide as your source of truth for what is in the exam. Go to https://aka.ms/examcode-studyguide (for example: https://aka.ms/az104-studyguide). Always reference it so you stay focused on relevant exam topics and avoid getting sidetracked.
		:::

	</TabItem>

	<TabItem label="Expert">
		# Expert
		:::note
		Best for experienced cloud professionals and architects, with a focus on technical and architectural depth across services, SKUs, and design decisions.
		:::

		:::caution
		Expert exams are role-based and test practical skills, not just course memorization. They can be very difficult without real hands-on experience with the products.
		:::

		:::tip
		Use the official study guide as your source of truth for what is in the exam. Go to https://aka.ms/examcode-studyguide (for example: https://aka.ms/az305-studyguide). Always reference it so you stay focused on relevant exam topics and avoid getting sidetracked.
		:::

	</TabItem>

	<TabItem label="Specialty">
		# Specialty
		:::note
		Best for professionals specializing in a specific Microsoft product area, with a focus on deep product-specific knowledge and how that product fits into the cloud ecosystem.
		:::

		:::caution
		Specialty exams are role-based and test practical skills, not just course memorization. They can be very difficult without real hands-on experience with the products.
